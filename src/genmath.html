Topic: Annuities

Introduction to Annuities

An annuity is a continuous stream of equal periodic payments from one party to another for a specified period of time to fulfill a financial obligation. Understanding annuities requires recognizing key characteristics such as regular payments, timing, and frequency.

Key Characteristics

  • Payments: Equal and periodic.
  • Timing: Occurs at either the beginning or end of an interval.
  • Frequency: Payment and interest rate compounding frequencies may align or differ.

Unlike single payments, solving series of payments requires selecting the correct formula based on the financial characteristics.

Types of Annuities

Annuities are classified based on the timing of payments and frequency of interest compounding. Below are the four types of annuities:

Ordinary Simple Annuity

Payments are made at the end of payment intervals, and the payment and compounding frequencies are equal. Example: Car loans with monthly payments and monthly interest compounding.

Ordinary General Annuity

Payments are made at the end of payment intervals, but the payment and compounding frequencies are unequal. Example: Mortgages with monthly payments and semi-annual interest compounding.

Simple Annuity Due

Payments are made at the beginning of payment intervals, with equal payment and compounding frequencies. Example: Car leases where the first payment is due at signing.

General Annuity Due

Payments are made at the beginning of payment intervals, but the payment and compounding frequencies are unequal. Example: RRSPs where monthly contributions have annual interest compounding.

Summary

Annuities must satisfy four characteristics: continuity, equal payment amounts, periodicity, and a specified time frame. If any characteristic is violated, it is not an annuity and requires different financial methods for calculations.
