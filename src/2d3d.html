Calculating the Straight-Line Distance

The shortest distance between two points is a straight line. Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can determine the straight-line distance when the path forms a right triangle. Consider walking 9 blocks east and 5 blocks north:

Formula: c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}

Substituting values: c = \sqrt{(9)^2 + (5)^2} = 10.3 blocks.

This result demonstrates that the straight-line distance (10.3 blocks) is considerably shorter than the 14 blocks walked.

Vector Representation

In two-dimensional motion, the path can be represented by vectors. The horizontal and vertical components of the motion add together to form the straight-line path. For instance:

  • A 9-block displacement east (horizontal component).
  • A 5-block displacement north (vertical component).
  • These vectors form a right triangle, with the hypotenuse representing the total displacement.

Independence of Motion

The horizontal and vertical components of two-dimensional motion are independent. For example, consider two baseballs: one dropped vertically and the other thrown horizontally. Despite differences in horizontal velocity, their vertical motions are identical, demonstrating that vertical and horizontal motions do not influence each other.
