Unit Conversion

A guide to understanding and applying unit conversions in various mathematical and everyday contexts.

What is Conversion of Units?

The use of a unit depends on the situation, such as the area of a room expressed in meters and the length of a pencil in centimeters. Converting one unit to another is necessary to ensure measurements are consistent for accurate calculations.

Length and Mass Conversion

Every physical quantity, like length and mass, has a specific unit. There are two systems of units: the SI (International System of Units) and the Metric system. For example, the SI unit of length is the meter (m), while other metric units include kilometer (km), centimeter (cm), and millimeter (mm).

Importance of Mathematical Conversions

Accurate conversions are essential to avoid confusion and ensure correctness. For example, we do not measure a pencil's length in kilometers, so it's necessary to convert between units like kilometers and centimeters for consistency in measurement.

Length Conversion Table

The relationship between units of length varies by multiples of 10, for example:

Unit Conversion Factor
1 millimeter0.001 meter
1 centimeter0.01 meter
1 decimeter0.1 meter
1 kilometer1000 meters
1 inch2.54 × 10⁻² meters
1 foot0.3048 meters
1 mile1.609344 km

Example: Convert 2 mm to dam

Given that 2 mm is to be converted to decameters (dam), the conversion factor from millimeters to decameters is 10^4. Thus, 2 mm = 2 ÷ 10⁴ dam = 0.0002 dam.

Points to Remember

To convert between units, remember:

  • To convert from larger to smaller units, multiply.
  • To convert from smaller to larger units, divide.

Unit Conversion Solved Examples

Example 1: Convert 2 cm to km. Answer: 2 cm = 2 ÷ 10⁵ km = 0.00002 km.

Example 2: Convert 5 grams to milligrams. Answer: 5 g = 5000 mg.
