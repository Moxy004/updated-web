Topic: Exponential Functions

What is Exponential Function?

An exponential function is a type of mathematical function that involves exponents. It has a constant base and a variable exponent. For example, f(x) = 2^x, where 2 is the base and x is the exponent. The exponential function is widely used in various fields like biology, finance, and physics.

Exponential Function Examples

Here are a few examples of exponential functions:

  • f(x) = 2^x
  • f(x) = (1/2)^x
  • f(x) = 3e^(2x)
  • f(x) = 4(3)^(-0.5x)

Exponential Growth

Exponential growth describes situations where a quantity increases at a rate proportional to its current value. For example, population growth, compounded interest, and bacterial growth are examples of exponential growth.

Exponential Decay

Exponential decay is the opposite of exponential growth. It refers to a situation where a quantity decreases at a rate proportional to its current value, such as radioactive decay or depreciation of assets.

Exponential Function Graphs

The graph of an exponential function can either show exponential growth or decay. For instance, the graph of f(x) = 2^x increases rapidly, while the graph of f(x) = (1/2)^x decreases as x increases.

Exponential Function Asymptotes

Exponential functions have horizontal asymptotes. The equation of the horizontal asymptote for a function of the form f(x) = ab^x + c is y = c. It represents the value that the function approaches as x becomes very large or very small.
